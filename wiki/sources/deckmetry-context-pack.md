# Deckmetry Context Pack (13-Document Strategy Package)

## Summary

A comprehensive 13-document strategy and product context package covering the Deckmetry opportunity with Wehrung's. Includes executive summary, go-to-market strategy, system overview, user flows, feature requirements, BOM/takeoff logic, data model, Epicor integration notes, pilot pricing, demo script, product positioning, open questions, and a master developer prompt.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Wehrung's is an active live opportunity** — They've already seen the prototype, showed strong interest, and want a follow-up meeting with IT + directors. Not hypothetical.
2. **Go-to-market is supplier-embedded** — Deckmetry lives on the supplier's website as two entry points (Homeowner / Contractor). Not a standalone consumer SaaS.
3. **Epicor is the ERP constraint** — Wehrung's runs Epicor. A deliberate 4-phase integration strategy was designed to avoid Epicor being a pilot blocker.
4. **IP ownership is a documented strategic concern** — Deckmetry retains platform IP. Wehrung's gets pilot access. Exclusivity requires a separate commercial agreement.
5. **Railing section logic detail** — Prefer 6 ft and 8 ft kit combinations with equal visual layout; avoid small leftover sections.
6. **Pilot is 60–90 days, paid** — Low-to-mid five figures. Success metrics: # contractor projects, # homeowner BOMs, # confirmed orders, avg order value, admin revision rate.
7. **Expansion roadmap** — Pergolas, covered porches, outdoor kitchens, fencing, paver patios, framing packages. Contractor repeat-order templates as sticky feature.

## Notable Data Points or Quotes

- Core positioning: *"Deckmetry helps building suppliers turn deck material sales into a guided digital ordering experience."*
- On Epicor: *"For the pilot, Deckmetry does not need to directly write into Epicor."*
- On IP: *"Do not give away too much ownership too early."*
- Waste factors specified: Decking 5–10%, Fascia 5–10%, Framing 5%, Railing = kit count not percentage.
- RDI Finyl Line white with black round balusters cited as known user preference.
- 6-stage status workflow: Draft → Requested → Received → Confirmed → Scheduled → Complete.

## Competitive Signals

- Deckmetry's strategic moat accumulates through supplier-specific catalogs, contractor-specific pricing, repeat contractor workflows, historical project data, ERP integrations, and homeowner lead channels.
- Competitors who rely on phone/email ordering are the direct displacement target.
- Making contractors fast enough that they don't price-shop other suppliers is the retention mechanism.
