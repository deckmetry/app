# 02 — Wehrung's Opportunity Strategy

## Current situation

Wehrung's has already seen the Deckmetry prototype and showed strong interest. They want another presentation with IT and directors. For that meeting, they specifically want to see realistic use scenarios for:

- Contractor Portal
- Homeowner Experience
- Admin/salesperson back portal

They currently use **Epicor** as their main business system.

## Recommended positioning for the next meeting

The next presentation should not be framed as “here is a deck calculator.” It should be framed as:

> A new digital sales and ordering channel for Wehrung's deck material business.

The message should be:
- This is not replacing their sales team.
- This is not bypassing their review process.
- This is not asking for full ERP integration on day one.
- This is creating an easier front door for homeowners and contractors, while still allowing Wehrung's to control pricing, availability, and fulfillment.

## Why Deckmetry is attractive for Wehrung's

### 1. It captures homeowners earlier

Many homeowners want to understand deck cost before speaking to a contractor. If they land on Wehrung's website and can build a deck concept, they are more likely to engage.

The tool creates:
- A material list.
- An estimated price.
- A lead record.
- An opportunity to request pro labor pricing.

This gives Wehrung's a new lead engine.

### 2. It gives contractors a reason to stay loyal

Contractors want speed. If a contractor can:
- Build a takeoff in minutes.
- See their own price.
- Request delivery.
- Save projects.
- Track order status.

Then Wehrung's becomes more than a supplier. Wehrung's becomes part of the contractor's operating system.

### 3. It reduces manual back-and-forth

Instead of scattered emails and incomplete material lists, Wehrung's receives structured requests with:
- Dimensions
- Selected brand/collection/color
- Deck height
- Railing details
- Stair details
- Framing assumptions
- BOM
- Requested delivery date
- Contractor/homeowner contact info

This reduces ambiguity.

### 4. It provides data

Over time, Wehrung's can see:
- Most selected brands
- Most common deck sizes
- Homeowner demand by area
- Contractor request volume
- Conversion rates
- Delivery demand patterns
- Products frequently quoted but not ordered

This data can help purchasing, sales, marketing, and inventory.

### 5. It protects human review

Deckmetry should not auto-commit Wehrung's inventory or delivery dates in the first version. It should create structured requests that Wehrung's reviews and confirms.

This avoids IT resistance and operational risk.

## How to handle Epicor in the meeting

The safest message:

> We know Wehrung's uses Epicor. For the pilot, we do not need to force a full integration on day one. The first version can generate structured requests, exportable BOMs, and admin-reviewed orders. During the pilot, we can work with IT to understand whether Epicor API access, import workflows, or CSV-based handoff makes the most sense.

## Business opportunity not to leave on the table

Deckmetry could become more than a Wehrung's-only tool.

Potential expansion models:
- Supplier SaaS platform for other lumberyards/building material dealers.
- White-labeled calculator embedded on supplier websites.
- Contractor portal tied to supplier-specific pricing.
- Homeowner lead marketplace connected to approved contractors.
- Material brand partnerships with Trex, TimberTech, Deckorators, RDI, etc.
- Paid contractor accounts for advanced project management, saved templates, and historical takeoffs.
- Analytics dashboard for supplier sales teams.

## Strategic recommendation

Do not give away too much ownership too early.

If Wehrung's funds the pilot, clarify whether they are paying for:
- A custom pilot for their location only.
- A white-labeled version of the platform.
- Exclusive rights in a territory.
- Or simply early access as a validation partner.

The best structure is likely:
- Wehrung's gets a discounted pilot and influence over product direction.
- Deckmetry retains ownership of the software/IP.
- Any full exclusivity requires a much larger commercial agreement.
