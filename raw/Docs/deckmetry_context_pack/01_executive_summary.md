# 01 — Executive Summary

## What Deckmetry is

Deckmetry is a web-based deck material takeoff, quoting, and order workflow platform designed for building material suppliers, starting with Wehrung's.

It allows homeowners and contractors to configure a deck using practical inputs, select materials, generate a bill of materials, see an estimated price or contractor-specific price, request labor pricing, request delivery, and move the order through a supplier-reviewed workflow.

## Why it matters

Currently, contractors often contact Wehrung's after they already have a takeoff ready. The workflow is usually manual:
1. Contractor creates the material list.
2. Contractor emails the BOM to Wehrung's.
3. Wehrung's checks pricing and availability.
4. Contractor asks for delivery options.
5. Internal teams coordinate manually.

Deckmetry compresses that process into a guided digital workflow. It can help Wehrung's capture demand earlier, standardize takeoffs, reduce email back-and-forth, and create a contractor portal that becomes difficult to replace.

## Why Wehrung's should care

Deckmetry can increase Wehrung's sales by:
- Capturing homeowner leads directly from their website.
- Turning homeowner curiosity into material lists and potential pro-installation requests.
- Making contractors more likely to buy from Wehrung's because ordering becomes faster.
- Giving contractors a repeatable project portal instead of email-only ordering.
- Creating better visibility into demand, project stages, delivery requests, and product preferences.
- Positioning Wehrung's as a tech-forward partner, not just a material supplier.

## Primary audiences

### Homeowners
Homeowners use Deckmetry as an embedded tool on Wehrung's website to create a basic deck concept, choose materials/colors, see an estimated material cost, receive a BOM by email, and request professional labor pricing.

### Contractors
Contractors use Deckmetry as a portal to generate takeoffs, view contractor-specific pricing, save projects, request delivery dates, and track project/order status.

### Wehrung's sales/admin team
Wehrung's uses an internal admin portal to review requests, verify availability, adjust pricing when needed, confirm delivery schedules, and manage leads/orders.

## Strategic opportunity

The initial Wehrung's pilot should not be positioned only as software development. It should be positioned as a **pilot validation partnership** around a new supplier-side sales channel.

The pilot should prove:
- Contractors will use the portal.
- Homeowners will submit lead requests.
- Wehrung's can shorten quote-to-order time.
- BOM accuracy is reliable enough for sales review.
- The system can eventually connect with Epicor or use exported data if API access is limited.

## Suggested pilot frame

A practical pilot could run for 60–90 days after launch, with a paid validation fee and limited scope. The pilot should test the core workflows with real users but avoid promising full ERP automation immediately.

Suggested pilot deliverables:
- Embedded website entry point.
- Homeowner configurator.
- Contractor project configurator.
- BOM generation.
- Basic contractor pricing logic.
- Admin review dashboard.
- Email notifications.
- Order/request status workflow.
- Exportable BOM/order data.
- Epicor integration discovery.

## Core positioning statement

Deckmetry helps building suppliers turn deck material sales into a guided digital ordering experience — giving homeowners instant clarity, contractors faster ordering, and supplier teams better control over pricing, availability, and fulfillment.
