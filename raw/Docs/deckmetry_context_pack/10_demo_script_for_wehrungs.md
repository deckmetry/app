# 10 — Demo Script for Wehrung's

## Meeting objective

Show Wehrung's IT and directors that Deckmetry is not just a calculator. It is a structured digital sales and ordering workflow for homeowners, contractors, and Wehrung's internal sales/admin users.

## Opening narrative

> Today we want to show how Deckmetry can work as a digital front door for Wehrung's deck material business. The goal is to help homeowners get clarity, help contractors move faster, and help Wehrung's receive cleaner, more structured material requests. This does not replace the Wehrung's team; it gives the team better information earlier in the process.

## Scenario 1 — Homeowner experience

### Setup
A homeowner visits Wehrung's website and wants to understand material cost for a new deck.

### Show
1. Wehrung's website entry point.
2. Two buttons: Homeowner and Contractor.
3. Click Homeowner.
4. Enter example deck:
   - 20 ft wide x 16 ft projection
   - Attached to house
   - Railing on front and sides
   - No stairs or simple stairs depending on demo
   - Composite decking
   - Railing selection
5. Select decking brand/collection/color.
6. Select railing type/color.
7. Generate BOM and estimated price.
8. Show lead capture.
9. Show “Request Pro Pricing for Labor.”
10. Show email/BOM confirmation.
11. Show admin receives the lead.

### Talking points
- Homeowner gets useful clarity.
- Wehrung's captures a lead.
- Wehrung's can connect the lead to a contractor.
- Pricing remains subject to review.

## Scenario 2 — Contractor portal

### Setup
A contractor already works with Wehrung's and wants to place a material request faster.

### Show
1. Contractor login.
2. Contractor dashboard.
3. Click New Project.
4. Enter jobsite and deck specs.
5. Select products.
6. Generate BOM.
7. Show contractor-specific pricing.
8. Request delivery date.
9. Submit to Wehrung's.
10. Show status becomes Requested.

### Talking points
- Contractor avoids creating takeoff manually.
- Wehrung's receives cleaner data.
- Contractor sees project/order status.
- Contractor-specific pricing can be supported.
- Wehrung's still confirms availability and delivery.

## Scenario 3 — Admin/sales portal

### Setup
Wehrung's sales/admin receives the contractor request.

### Show
1. Admin dashboard.
2. New requests list.
3. Open contractor request.
4. Review project inputs.
5. Review BOM line items.
6. Edit/adjust quantity if needed.
7. Confirm availability.
8. Confirm or propose delivery date.
9. Move status from Requested to Received/Confirmed/Scheduled.
10. Show notification back to contractor.

### Talking points
- Admin remains in control.
- Pricing and availability are not automatically final.
- The request is structured.
- The team can process it faster than scattered email.

## Epicor discussion script

> We know Epicor is central to Wehrung's operations. Our recommendation is not to make Epicor integration a blocker for the pilot. The first version can generate clean BOMs, structured requests, and exportable data. During the pilot, we can work with IT to evaluate the best integration path, whether that is API, CSV import, product catalog sync, or another workflow.

## Key closing message

> The pilot is designed to validate whether this creates measurable value: more homeowner leads, faster contractor ordering, cleaner internal requests, and better visibility for Wehrung's sales team. If the pilot proves those points, then we can define the deeper integration and full rollout.
